### PÁGINA 1 — "De Cima, De Frente, De Lado!"
**Objetivo pedagógico:** A criança reconhece as três visões — vertical, frontal e oblíqua — e compreende que o mesmo objeto pode ser representado de formas diferentes dependendo do ângulo de observação.

**PROMPT:**
```
Brazilian children's educational comic book page, 4 panels in 2x2 grid,
colorful flat illustration, bold black outlines, warm amber and golden yellow palette,
Portuguese speech bubbles, thick panel borders, white gutters.
Warm sunlit classroom atmosphere with big windows letting in golden morning light.

Panel 1 (top left):
Bright classroom interior, large windows on the left wall showing a sunny courtyard outside,
rows of amber-yellow desks with small chairs, green chalkboard on the back wall with
"COMO VER O MUNDO?" written in white chalk letters.
LIS (7-year-old Brazilian girl, warm light skin, long wavy chestnut-brown hair, large brown eyes,
denim overalls over white t-shirt, white sneakers with amber laces): standing near the front of
the classroom, facing slightly toward the reader, arms loosely at her sides, head tilted up
with an expression of genuine curiosity — eyebrows raised, eyes wide and sparkling, mouth open
in a small "ooh" of anticipation.
VIU (small amber-gold quadcopter drone, large circular camera-lens eye, tiny cartographer's hat
on top, four golden propellers spinning): hovering at Lis's shoulder level, his big lens-eye
aimed directly at her, propellers spinning at a medium pace. He has just materialized or flown
in through the window — a small amber glow trail behind him suggests arrival.
Light streaming through windows casts warm golden bars across the classroom floor.
Speech bubble VIU (cheerful, the letters vibrating slightly to suggest his buzzing voice):
"Lis! Vou te mostrar algo incrível!"
Speech bubble LIS (curious, eyebrow raised): "O quê, Viu? O quê?"

Panel 2 (top right):
Close-up bird's-eye view (VISÃO VERTICAL) of a simple wooden school desk from directly above,
occupying the center of the panel. The desk is shown as a flat amber-brown rectangle with
clean edges. Lis's small hands are visible at the very bottom of the panel, gripping the
desk's edge from below — suggesting she is standing underneath looking up, though the main
focus is the top-down shape of the desk.
Around the desk, small amber arrows point downward (↓) from the four sides, reinforcing the
"looking from above" concept.
In the top-right corner of the panel: a small golden badge/label shaped like a cloud, with
bold black text: "VISÃO VERTICAL" and a tiny icon of an eye looking straight down (👁️ ↓).
The background behind the desk: a clean cream-white floor seen from above, with faint grid
lines suggesting a map or plan view.
VIU is shown from above as a small amber-gold square with four propeller arms extended,
hovering directly over the desk — he is demonstrating the vertical view position.
Speech bubble VIU (coming from his position above, pointing down with a tiny digital arrow
projected from his lens): "De CIMA para BAIXO — assim!"
No speech bubble for Lis in this panel — her reaction carries into Panel 3.

Panel 3 (bottom left):
Same wooden school desk, now shown in FRONTAL VIEW — the desk is seen straight-on from the
front, showing its flat top surface as a narrow horizontal band and its two front legs clearly
visible below. The desk's front face is the main element, centered in the panel.
Lis stands directly in front of the desk, body facing forward (toward the viewer), head level
with the desk surface. Her expression: eyes wide with dawning understanding, one hand raised
with index finger pointing at the desk surface excitedly — she is connecting the concept.
VIU hovers at Lis's left side, his body now tilted slightly so his lens-eye is perfectly level
with the desk surface — demonstrating the frontal view position.
Small horizontal arrows (←→) on both sides of the desk reinforce the "straight from the front"
concept. In the top-left corner: a small label badge: "VISÃO FRONTAL" with an eye icon
looking straight ahead (👁️ →).
Warm amber light from the side window illuminates Lis and the desk.
Speech bubble VIU (his buzzing letters, enthusiastic): "Agora DE FRENTE!"
Speech bubble LIS (excited, finger pointing): "É a frente da carteira!"

Panel 4 (bottom right):
Same wooden school desk, now shown in OBLIQUE VIEW — seen from above and to the side
simultaneously, the desk appears three-dimensional: its top surface is visible AND one side
face is visible AND the front is partially visible. This is a classic 3/4 isometric view.
The desk looks fully three-dimensional and solid.
Lis stands to the right and slightly behind the desk, head at an angle looking at it from
above-and-to-the-side. Her expression: huge grin showing teeth, both hands raised with
thumbs up — she has understood all three views.
VIU hovers at the upper-left corner of the desk, positioned diagonally — his body tilted at
a 45-degree angle to demonstrate the oblique viewing position. His propellers spin fast with
excitement, small golden sparkles radiating outward.
Diagonal arrows (↗ and ↘) around the desk reinforce the "from the side AND above" concept.
In the top-right corner: label badge: "VISÃO OBLÍQUA" with an eye icon looking diagonally
(👁️ ↗).
Caption box at bottom of panel, amber background with dark text: "Três visões, três formas
de ver o mesmo lugar!"
Speech bubble LIS (triumphant, arms raised): "Três visões diferentes!"
Speech bubble VIU (propellers spinning fast, lens sparkling): "Agora você já sabe!"

Style: warm amber and golden classroom tones, flat illustration, bold black outlines,
grid-map background elements, Portuguese speech bubbles with black borders, 2x2 grid,
thick panel borders, white gutters. Each panel's view badge (vertical/frontal/oblique)
should be clearly legible in amber/gold/cream colors.
```

## DICAS PARA MELHORES RESULTADOS

- **Viu** é o personagem mais difícil de manter consistente. O segredo está em dois traços:
  (1) o chapéuzinho de cartógrafo no topo — pequeno, bege/cáqui, um pouco torto; e
  (2) a lente-câmera redonda no centro — é seu único "olho" e precisa ter expressão clara.
  Regenere a folha de personagens até ter uma versão sólida antes de gerar as páginas.
- **Página 2, Painel 2** (o split comparando planta × maquete) é o painel mais difícil —
  ele exige que dois elementos coexistam lado a lado com estilos visuais distintos (flat
  diagram vs. 3D cardboard model). Se o DALL·E comprimir demais, gere os dois lados
  separadamente com prompts focados e combine no Canva.
- Para consistência entre páginas: adicione ao início de cada novo prompt o bloco
  "Character reference: same characters as previous page — LIS: 7yo girl, chestnut wavy
  hair, denim overalls, amber sneakers; VIU: amber-gold drone, large lens-eye, khaki hat,
  four golden propellers."
- Se o texto dos balões sair em inglês, corrija no **Canva** adicionando caixas de texto
  sobre as imagens geradas.
- **DALL·E 3** funciona melhor para cenas de personagens, sala de aula e ao ar livre.
  **Ideogram** funciona melhor para o Painel 2 da Página 2 (o split planta/maquete) e
  para a Página 4 Painel 2 (o notebook com o mapa desenhado).
- A cor âmbar (#F59E0B) e dourado (#FCD34D) devem ser as dominantes — mantenha essa
  paleta quente ao longo de todas as 4 páginas para consistência visual do tema.
